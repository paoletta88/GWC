Level 1 - Where do we go now?

15m 00s

You have to know, where you need to go!

Getting in using unique pass-phrases pre-dates IT systems by atleast 3000 years. Even ancient tribal socities had unique passwords to control who was allowed to get in at the gates. Computer systems are slightly more evolved than tribal societies and are used by more people than your secret club members. Authentication, authorization and tracking of all users became complicated as computing evolved and a new way to create a system of record that controlled access and kept logs was required. The words log-in or sign-in come from many historical references, but mean the same thing - securing the door.

This page though, is not about secure doors and strong locks, it is about the doors themselves. Even if you knew the password, you had to KNOW where to use it. So think about getting into secure systems and how to get to the door. How does one get anywhere on the internet?

Random Etymology
All nautical instruments that measure the speed of a ship through water are known as LOGS. This nomenclature dates back to the days of sail, when sailors tossed a log attached to a rope knotted at regular intervals off the stern of a ship. Sailors counted the number of knots that passed through their hands in a given time to determine the ship's speed.
Did you know?
We've all used the word URL in context of web-addresses, but what is a URL?
URL stands for Unique Resource Locator. It is a reference to a resource that specifies the location of the resource on the ccomputer network and a mechanism for retrieving it. The Uniform Resource Locator was standardized in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee who is also considered the father of the World Wide Web.
